Bi-Weekly Financial Vocabulary

New terms are added on a rolling basis so you can build confidence step by step.

Investing Term Simple Explanation Real-World Example
P/E Ratio (Price-to-Earnings) Measures how much investors are willing to pay per dollar of company earnings. A higher P/E often reflects growth expectations. If a company trades at a P/E of 20, investors pay $20 for every $1 of earnings. High-growth tech firms often have higher P/Es.
Short Squeeze When heavily shorted stocks rise sharply, forcing short sellers to buy shares to cover losses which pushes the price up even more. GameStop (2021) soared as retail traders triggered a squeeze, causing hedge funds to close short positions at large losses.
Market Capitalization The total value of a company’s shares (share price × shares outstanding). Often used to group companies by size. A company worth $10 billion is considered mid-cap, while giants like Apple exceed $2 trillion.
Liquidity How easily an asset can be bought or sold without significantly affecting its price. AAPL stock is highly liquid, while a rare collectible coin is not and might take time to sell at a fair price.
Volatility Measures how much price moves within a period. High volatility means larger, more frequent swings. Cryptocurrencies often exhibit high volatility compared to bonds or large blue-chip stocks.
